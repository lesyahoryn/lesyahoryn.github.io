Lesya Horyn

Postdoctoral Researcher | Fermilab

I am a particle physics researcher on the CMS Experiment with the Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory. I work on the Outer Tracker upgrade and searches for new physics via precision measurements. I got my PhD from the University of Chicago where I was a member of the ATLAS Experiment. I searched for new long-lived particles (2011.07812) and worked on the Fast TracKer, a hardware-based tracking upgrade to the ATLAS trigger system (2101.05078). I also work to make physics a place where everyone is welcome and can succeed. I served on the ATLAS Early Career Scientist Board. I served as a an executive committee member of the American Physical Society's Forum on Graduate Student Affairs, and was a student delegate to the AAAS Science and Human Rights Coalition.
